Anatomy of the Table

The periodic table is a 2-D map of the elements arranged by atomic number (Z), with chemically similar elements aligned in vertical columns (groups) and elements with the same outer-shell number (n) in horizontal rows (periods).

Named Groups and Their Common Behavior

Group 1 — Alkali metals (Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Fr) — Very reactive metals; form +1 cations; react vigorously with water to give H₂.

Group 2 — Alkaline-earth metals (Be, Mg, Ca, Sr, Ba, Ra) — Reactive metals; form +2 cations; less violent with water than Group 1.

Group 17 — Halogens (F, Cl, Br, I, At) — Reactive nonmetals; form −1 anions; diatomic in elemental form (F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂).

Group 18 — Noble gases (He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn) — Full valence shells (ns² np⁶ except He = 1s²); essentially unreactive under ordinary conditions.

Transition metals (d-block, Groups 3–12) — Often form multiple cation charges (Fe²⁺/Fe³⁺, Cu⁺/Cu²⁺); colored compounds; many catalytic uses.

Lanthanides and actinides (f-block) — Used in magnets, lasers, and nuclear technology; radioactive past Z = 83.
