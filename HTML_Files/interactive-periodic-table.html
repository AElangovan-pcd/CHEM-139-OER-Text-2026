About this resource

This interactive periodic table is provided as an open educational resource for chemistry curricula. Pauling electronegativity values (χ), first ionization energies, and electron affinities are based on standard references including the IUPAC Periodic Table, the NIST Atomic Spectra Database, and the CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics. Ground-state electron configurations follow IUPAC ground-state assignments; for elements 104 and beyond, configurations are theoretical predictions that have not been experimentally verified.

Some elements have no widely accepted value for one or more properties and are shown as —unavailable. This applies to the lighter noble gases (He, Ne, Ar) for χ, to elements that do not form a stable mononegative anion for electron affinity (most noble gases, Be–Mg, Mn, Zn, Cd, Hg, and several lanthanides), and to most superheavy synthetic elements (Z ≥ 104), whose chemistry has not been studied in sufficient detail to assign reliable scale values.
