Een [femtometer](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/femtometer "af:femtometer") (wit stafie) word teenoor die [atoomkern](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/atoomkern "af:atoomkern") aangedui, en een [ångströmlengte](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%85ngstr%C3%B6m_(eenheid) "af:Ångström (eenheid)") (swart stafie) teenoor die hele atoom. Die donkerte van die [elektron](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/elektron "af:elektron") [wolk](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/elektronwolk "af:elektronwolk") stem met die siglyn-integraal oor die [waarskynlikheidsfunksie](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/waarskynlikheidsfunksie "af:waarskynlikheidsfunksie") van die elektron se 1s- [atoomorbitaal](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/atoomorbitaal "af:atoomorbitaal") ooreen. Die vergrote [kern](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/atoomkern "af:atoomkern") is skematies en toon [protone](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/proton "af:proton") in rooi en [neutrone](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/neutron "af:neutron") in pers. In werklikheid is die golffunksie van die kern soos elkeen van die [nukleone](https://af.wikipedia.org/wiki/nukleon "af:nukleon") ook sferies simmetries en 1s, en die vier deeltjies, elk met 'n ander kwantumgetal, sal soos die elektrone almal in dieselfde mees waarskynlike ruimte verkeer, naamlik in die presiese middelpunt van die kern. Vir meer komplekse kerne is dit egter nie die geval nie. Met dank aan Åke Back.<br>**English:** A depiction of the atomic structure of the [helium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/helium "w:helium") atom. The darkness of the [electron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/electron "w:electron") [cloud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/electron_cloud "w:electron cloud") corresponds to the line-of-sight integral over the [probability function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/probability_function "w:probability function") of the 1s [atomic orbital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/atomic_orbital "w:atomic orbital") of the electron. The magnified [nucleus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/atomic_nucleus "w:atomic nucleus") is schematic, showing [protons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/proton "w:proton") in red and [neutrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/neutron "w:neutron") in purple. In reality, the nucleus (and the wavefunction of each of the [nucleons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/nucleon "w:nucleon")) is also spherically symmetric and 1s, and the four particles, each with a different quantum number, like the electrons in the helium atom, are all most likely to be found in the same space, at the exact center of the nucleus. (For more complicated nuclei this is not the case. Thanks to Åke Back.) |
